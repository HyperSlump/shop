## Why this plate works in busy sessions
The early reflection shape stays focused, so your dry source remains legible while the tail adds density behind it. With moderate pre-delay and conservative damping, vocals stay forward even when the reverb is obvious.

In practical terms, I can leave one default send in the template and quickly adapt decay, color, and modulation per song. That reduces setup fatigue and keeps decisions musical.

## Working preset map
Use this as a fast baseline:

- Drums: decay 1.9 to 2.3 s, pre-delay 20 to 35 ms.
- Vocals: decay 3.2 to 4.1 s, pre-delay 45 to 65 ms.
- Keys and pads: decay 2.5 to 3.5 s, low-pass return around 10.5 kHz.
- Always high-pass reverb return around 160 to 220 Hz.

## A/B notes against other plate tools
- Little Plate: instant vibe, less parameter depth.
- EMT style plugins: richer mass, often heavier CPU.
- Stock DAW plates: usable, usually need more filtering work.

> A useful plate should feel easy to keep and easy to remove. If bypassing it makes the mix better, it is too loud.
